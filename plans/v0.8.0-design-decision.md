## 1. Where #71 actually stands after M3, and what the v1.0 note changes

**M3 fixed the acute thing #71 was filed about.** Issue #71 was filed against an `O(folds √ó document size)` resend cost: legacy reflection re-sends a capped head prefix of `reflections.md` on every fold, so total resend grows super-linearly and tail sections past the head cap are never surfaced. M3's section-targeted reflection breaks the `√ó size` factor ‚Äî per-fold context is now a small fixed core bundle plus only the touched H3 entries (`_section_targeted_context`, ceilinged at 9k), the rotation fallback guarantees coverage reaches past the head, and reassembly is byte-faithful and fail-closed. The expensive, correctness-eroding behavior #71 named no longer happens at default settings. This is real and verified.

**The residual #71 problems split sharply into REAL and THEORETICAL at this product's scale.** The product is *personal, host-local, cross-agent Markdown memory* ‚Äî not a hyperscale store. The measured corpus matters here: Bryan's actual `reflections.md` is **37 KB total**, largest section `Active Projects` at **27 KB**, largest H3 entry **10 KB / 67 lines** ‚Äî half the prompt's own soft 120-line target, and below the ~48 KB threshold that even *activates* sectioned routing. Against that ground truth:

- **(a) fat sections / (e) sub-fact addressability ‚Äî largely THEORETICAL today.** The 100x fixture deliberately holds `_PER_SECTION_CHARS=4000` constant and scales count, because that reflects the real corpus shape. A single H3 ballooning to the point where re-sending it whole dominates is years away and is itself a sign of pathological reflector behavior the prompt already discourages.
- **(d) total corpus growth / no compaction ‚Äî REAL but non-acute.** The corpus accretes monotonically with no archival. At 37 KB this is not biting, but it is the residual that *will* bite first at personal scale, and it has catastrophic blast radius if ever mishandled (reflections are the irreplaceable asset).
- **(b) per-fact provenance ‚Äî REAL and twice-demanded.** #71's own acceptance criteria require "every fact traceable to source observations" and per-section provenance readable in `reflections.md`. This is the one residual with a concrete, stated acceptance criterion still unmet.
- **(c) conflict detection in the sectioned path ‚Äî REAL, cheap, and dormant.** `find_reflection_conflicts` is already built and tested but is never called from `_reflect_sectioned`.
- **Fold count still scales with observation volume** ‚Äî unsolved by everyone here, tolerable because per-fold cost is now bounded and observations age out.

**The v1.0 note reframes v0.8.0 from "finish #71" to "spend the slot wisely across several pulls."** `plans/team-memory-direction.md` ¬ß6 prescribes *cheap, reversible, no-1.0-work-committed* "keep-the-door-open" moves: carry provenance (`owner/scope/source/team`) on `Document`/`SearchResult`; generalize `filter_reflection_entries_for_cluster` into a `local‚Üíteam‚Üíorg` scope pipeline; keep recall multi-backend-aware; prototype scope-aware merge against *local* indexes only. The grounding's strongest finding is that **provenance-on-the-memory-unit is the same primitive demanded from both ends** ‚Äî #71's "traceable to source" and ¬ß6.3's typed provenance are one schema. Meanwhile #81 (recall quality + talk robustness) shows the *current shipped product* is weak where users actually touch it (recall/talk), with bm25 reproducibly beating an under-embedded qmd-hybrid. So v0.8.0 is a contest between: a #71 scaling slice, the ¬ß6 door-open substrate, and #81 quality fixes ‚Äî and the winning path advances more than one at once.

## 3. Per-option summary

**Option A ‚Äî Full memory-unit store.**
- Strongest design: the *only* option that closes the entire M3 residual surface (a/b/c/d/e) with one provenance schema serving both #71 and ¬ß6.3.
- Reuses the right things (reflection_metadata schema, sync/ append-log patterns, materialize‚Üírefresh) ‚Äî but must refactor `ClusterStore` to be crypto-optional/cluster-optional, an L touching the most rule-encumbered module to build a feature meant to be host-local.
- **Fatal critique:** its value and its safety constraint are mutually exclusive. Supersession edges + stable-across-edit IDs have nowhere to live except an authoritative store ‚Üí Markdown stops being the full source of truth ‚Üí breaks the inspectability guarantee and puts the irreplaceable asset at max blast radius. Every mitigation that protects durable memory neutralizes the reason to build it.
- No user-visible value until M6 (~3 increments deep), gated on a recall layer #81 shows could *regress* under unit-chunking.

**Option B ‚Äî Compaction-first.**
- Strongest design: attacks (d), the only residual with catastrophic blast radius, using the proven byte-faithful parse/reassemble model plus existing `prune_stale_snapshots`.
- **Fatal critique:** proposes destructive rewrites of a 37 KB asset to relieve growth that doesn't exist yet. "Metadata carry-forward across an LLM reword" is the whole problem (content-hash IDs change on edit; no alignment oracle), mis-sized as M. scope=local content can leak when merged into a cluster bullet (line-level filter can't catch it).
- **B0 (measurement/diagnostics) is the salvageable piece** ‚Äî pure, zero-blast-radius, the honest way to discover whether (d) is real before acting.

**Option C ‚Äî Provenance-lite on sectioned reflection.**
- Strongest design: delivers (b) and (c) by reusing dormant machinery ‚Äî `find_reflection_conflicts` exists but is never called in the fold path; the inline schema, IDs, scope filter, TTL all already ship. No new store.
- **Sharpest critiques:** per-fact `source_obs` pointers become *dangling references* the moment observations age out (the steady state) ‚Äî provenance that rots by design. C2 (fold-time stamping of only changed bullets) is mis-sized as M; the linkage from "which bullet changed / which chunk produced it" to the post-hoc whole-doc `ensure_reflection_metadata` pass doesn't exist and is an L research problem on the byte-faithful path. A `derived_from=<local-obs-id>` field on a `scope=cluster` bullet leaks the existence of local source material.
- The winning slice: lead with C3 conflict surfacing as a read-only one-shot pass; make any provenance section-level/rot-proof, not per-fact-pointer.

**Option D ‚Äî Harden M3 and stop.**
- Strongest design: the only option that refuses to let an issue number auto-authorize a rewrite. Correctly identifies that the acute problem is solved and durable memory is the asset you must not risk. Lowest-blast-radius store answer (no store).
- **Sharpest critiques:** it crowns fat-sections (the *rare* residual) as keystone while deferring (d) (the *common* one); the NEW_AFTER-split routing bias collides with the H3-drop guard (either does nothing or erodes byte-faithfulness); Inc 2's "real multi-MB corpus" gate has no real multi-MB corpus to run on.
- The decisive critique correction: **D conflates provenance with the store.** Inline, Markdown-authoritative provenance is an S/M, not the L D pretends ‚Äî so "decline provenance" is an unforced loss.

**Option E ‚Äî Door-open / governance-seed first.**
- Strongest design: the only option that advances *both* #71 and the v1.0 substrate with near-zero new risk surface ‚Äî builds the shared provenance primitive, changes the one bottleneck both roadmaps name (`RecallEngine` single-backend), seeds the scope lattice in the exact file ¬ß5 designates, and retires #81 ‚Äî **with no datastore**.
- **Sharpest critiques:** E3 fusion backend (L) rests on n=1, under-embedded #81 evidence ‚Äî you'd spend the L before you can run the gate. E2's "inert" `team`/`org` scope vocabulary is a latent cluster-leak: today's filter only drops `=="local"`, so `scope=team` would silently sync as `cluster`. E1 plumbing has no consumer until the forbidden team tier or a benchmark-gated default flip.
- **E4 talk fixes are a clean, grounded, independent win** that should ship regardless.

**Where C and E overlap ‚Äî and whether they merge: YES, merge them.** Both build *provenance-on-the-memory-unit*, both choose *no new store*, both grow the scope filter in `reflection_metadata.py`, both reuse the `moss.py` inline‚Üîtyped round-trip pattern. C reasons at the bullet/inline level and adds conflict surfacing in the reflect path; E reasons at the Document/SearchResult level and adds multi-backend recall + scope lattice + #81. They are the *same investment viewed from the two ends the grounding says one path serves*. The reconciling design is exactly what both critiques converged on independently: **inline Markdown metadata is authoritative (C's auditability, #71's criterion), typed copies ride on `Document`/`SearchResult` (E's mergeability, ¬ß6.3).** The merged option is the recommendation.

## 4. Recommendation ‚Äî the v0.8.0 scope

**Recommended path: ship a merged C‚à©E "provenance + scope-seed + recall-quality" v0.8.0, plus B0 measurement to inform a *future* compaction decision. Defer the full unit store (A) indefinitely. Defer compaction (B) behind data. Decline destructive and speculative work.**

This is the smallest reversible step that advances *both* #71 and the v1.0 trajectory, while honoring every product-thesis constraint (Markdown inspectability, personal-scale realism, fail-closed safety, gate discipline) and refusing to bet the irreplaceable asset.

### IN for v0.8.0 (sequenced)

1. **#81 talk + recall-quality fixes (E4) ‚Äî ship first, independent.** Distinguish "recall timed out / unavailable" from "no relevant memory found" in both the spoken turn and `--json`; add `OM_TALK_RECALL_TIMEOUT` (replace the hardcoded 8s in `Conversation._recall_for`). Build the labeled query‚Üíexpected-doc benchmark in `observational-memory-bench` (n‚â•5, bootstrap CIs, Holm-corrected permutation tests). **Quick win, zero dependency on the substrate, retires the most user-felt gap.** [S/M]

2. **Conflict surfacing in the sectioned path (C3, narrowed) ‚Äî read-only, one-shot.** Wire the already-built `find_reflection_conflicts` into a single `om reflect --check-conflicts` pass over the *final reassembled doc* (never per-fold ‚Äî per-fold reintroduces the O(folds√ódoc) shape M3 killed). Emit a throwaway review artifact like cluster materialization already does. No mutation of durable Markdown, no fold-path surgery. [M]

3. **Provenance + scope substrate (merged C1/C5 + E1/E2) ‚Äî the shared primitive, built the cheap way.** Add typed `owner`/`scope`/`source`/`source-window` fields to `Document`/`SearchResult`, populated from inline `<!--om:-->` via the `moss.py` round-trip pattern (E1/C5). Generalize `filter_reflection_entries_for_cluster` into a `local‚Üíteam‚Üíorg` pipeline with **fail-closed allowlist semantics: anything not explicitly `cluster` is dropped at the boundary** (fixes E2's latent leak); `team`/`org` defined but with no tier consuming them. 4. **B0 measurement-only diagnostic ‚Äî instrument, don't act.** Add section/H3 size + coldness measurement to `om doctor` / a `--quality-report`. Pure, read-only, zero blast radius. Run it for a release so the *next* compaction decision is data-grounded instead of speculative. [S]

5. **Multi-backend-aware RecallEngine ‚Äî seam only, fusion benchmark-gated.** Make `RecallEngine` *capable* of merging N backends (the ¬ß6.1/¬ß6.4 + #71 indexed-retrieval bottleneck), but **do not build the fusion ranking until the #81 benchmark (item 1) justifies it.** Default unchanged (single backend). This inverts E3's "spend the L before you can fail the gate." [S for the seam; fusion deferred]

### OUT for v0.8.0 ‚Äî and why

- **Full memory-unit store (A) ‚Äî OUT, deferred indefinitely.** Internally contradictory (safety cancels value), targets residuals the fixtures dodge and no corpus establishes, forces a security-sensitive ClusterStore refactor for a host-local feature, no value until 3 increments deep. The provenance demand it justifies itself with is met by item 3 at a fraction of the cost.
- **Destructive compaction B1‚ÄìB4 ‚Äî OUT, gated behind B0 data.** At 37 KB the problem is absent; B builds destructive rewrites of the irreplaceable asset with no preservation oracle and a scope=local merge-leak. Revisit *only if* B0 shows real sections crossing real thresholds on real corpora.
- **Per-fact provenance pointers / fold-time stamping (C2/C4) ‚Äî OUT.** Mis-sized L research problem on the freshly-stabilized byte-faithful path, producing pointers that rot. Section-level rot-proof provenance (item 3) meets the acceptance criterion honestly.
- **#54 Obsidian ‚Äî OUT.** Backlog/community signal, not scheduled, no acceptance pressure. The provenance/scope work in item 3 incidentally keeps the materialized-view-exporter door open without committing.
- **#77 async-Batch model validation ‚Äî OUT of the architecture slot** (it's an independent P3 bug fix that can ride any release; not part of this decision).

**Why this scope wins.** It advances #71 (provenance criterion met; conflict detection live; indexed-retrieval seam ready) AND the v1.0 substrate (¬ß6.1/6.2/6.3 hooks seeded) AND the current product (#81 quality) ‚Äî three pulls, one slot. It builds **no new datastore**, so it inherits none of the SQLite/JSONL/crypto-refactor/migration/redaction risk, and the catastrophic-corruption failure mode is structurally absent. It keeps the v1.0 door open (scope lattice, multi-backend seam, typed provenance) without building one byte of the 1.0 cloud/team tier (¬ß7 honored; trust-model decision firewalled). It is the smallest reversible step: every increment is additive, default-preserving, and independently revertible.

## 5. The open questions ‚Äî resolved or framed

**#71/M4 set:**

- **(i) Opt-in cadence.** *Mostly moot ‚Äî no store to gate.* Section-level provenance is additive inline metadata on a path that already runs; conflict surfacing is an explicit `--check-conflicts` flag (opt-in) before any automatic behavior; the multi-backend seam ships behind a backend-selector default-OFF. No subsystem to disable, so the `OM_USAGE_TRACKING=0` "skip creation" requirement is N/A. **Resolved.**
- **(ii) SQLite vs JSONL vs cluster-record.** *NONE ‚Äî build no store.* This is the defining property of the recommended path. State stays inline-authoritative in `reflections.md`; the only derived artifacts are the already-rebuildable search index and the throwaway conflict-review file. Sidesteps the "duplicate ClusterStore unless refactored crypto-optional" risk entirely. **Resolved.**
- **(iii) Cluster sharing timing.** *Deferred by construction.* No new record kind, so nothing new crosses the allowlist-based transport. The generalized scope filter *prepares* the enforcement boundary (fail-closed: non-`cluster` dropped) but no team tier is built. **Resolved.**
- **(iv) Metadata visibility.** *Inline Markdown authoritative; typed copies on Document/SearchResult.* The reconciling design the grounding recommends and the `moss.py` pattern already implements. Fully honors Markdown-inspectability. **Resolved.**
- **(v) `om memory` CLI timing.** *Read-only and thin, or deferred.* No unit store ‚áí no add/edit/delete verbs. A read-only `om recall --explain` / provenance-walk surface is the most to add; the full `om memory` CLI waits for a store that may never be built. **Resolved.**

**v1.0-relevant questions v0.8.0 touches:**

- **Does the scope lattice live in `reflection_metadata` or a separate ACL layer?** *Recommended: grow it in `reflection_metadata.py` for v0.8.0* (where the binary `local|cluster` filter already lives and where ¬ß5 designates). Tradeoff: a richer `local‚Üíteam‚Üíorg‚Üíper-project` lattice with audit/retention may eventually outgrow inline-metadata-and-filter-functions and want a dedicated ACL/governance pass. **Needs Bryan's call at the 1.0 boundary, not now** ‚Äî v0.8.0 only seeds the vocabulary with fail-closed semantics, which is reversible either way.
- **Do provenance-on-units and provenance-on-SearchResult share one model?** *Recommended: yes ‚Äî one schema, inline-authoritative, typed copies on retrieval objects.* This is the single strongest "one path serves both" finding and the merged C‚à©E rests on it. Tradeoff: if a real unit store ever lands (A revived), section-level inline provenance is a *weaker* substrate than typed records and would need migration ‚Äî but it's forward-compatible (the fields survive) and far cheaper now. **Resolved for v0.8.0; revisit only if a unit store is ever justified by data.**

## 6. Grill-me prep ‚Äî the sharpest unresolved tensions

1. **The central one: does the full memory-unit store (A) earn its place at all post-M3?** My answer is *no, not now and possibly never* ‚Äî provenance (item 3) + conflict surfacing (item 2) + future data-gated compaction (B0‚ÜíB?) cover the *real* need, and A's value/safety contradiction is structural. Where this is fragile: if the corpus genuinely grows to multi-MB with fat sections over the next year, addressability (e) and compaction (d) become real, and a unit store *might* be the clean answer ‚Äî but that's a future bet to make on future data, not now.

2. **Is section-level rot-proof provenance an honest satisfaction of #71's "every fact traceable to source observations," or a dodge?** I claim `derived_from_obs_window=<date-range>` at section level is honest *because* observations age out ‚Äî per-fact pointers would dangle. A reasonable person could argue #71 literally said "every fact" and a date-window is weaker than per-fact lineage. The counter: per-fact lineage that rots is *worse* than honest coarse lineage. This is the most defensible-but-debatable scoping call.

3. **Is conflict surfacing (item 2) worth a slot, or is it noise?** `find_reflection_conflicts` is heuristic (normalized-text-differs by section/kind). On a personal single-node corpus the bucketing key is cluster-shaped (record_id/node_id) and needs rebuilding, and false-positives could train users to ignore the warning. Push: is the dormant machinery actually cheap to adapt, or is "wire it in" hiding real work?

4. **Does deferring compaction (B) mean we ship v0.8.0 with #71's hardest residual (d) still open and growing?** Yes. I argue B0 measurement is the honest gate and 37 KB doesn't justify destructive rewrites. The fragility: if Bryan's corpus or a heavy user's grows fast, we've deferred the one thing with catastrophic blast radius. Counter: deferring is reversible; destroying memory isn't.

5. **The multi-backend seam (item 5) with fusion deferred ‚Äî are we building plumbing with no consumer?** E's critique applies to my own recommendation. The seam has value only if fusion ships, and fusion is benchmark-gated on #81 which is itself new work. Honest risk: item 5 could be motion, not progress, if the benchmark shows single-backend is fine. Mitigation: it's an S, and the #81 benchmark (item 1) decides it within the same release.

6. **Does the scope lattice (`team`/`org` defined but inert) create more risk than it removes?** E's sharpest finding: an inert vocabulary on the `scope=local`-must-not-leak boundary is dangerous. I've mandated fail-closed allowlist semantics (drop non-`cluster`) to fix it ‚Äî but adding *any* unenforced vocabulary to the most safety-critical filter is arguably premature. Reasonable position: don't add `team`/`org` until a tier consumes them; just keep the filter generalizable.

7. **Are we letting "personal scale = 37 KB" justify too much deferral?** The whole "do less" thesis leans on one user's current corpus. If the product succeeds and corpora grow 10‚Äì100x organically (not synthetically), the residuals I call theoretical become real fast. Where the analysis is weakest: I have *one* real corpus measurement and a fixture that admits it doesn't allocate real bytes. The honest hedge is B0 ‚Äî instrument so we'd *see* the growth before it bites.
