## 2. Recommendation: one release, two headlines; save the mail splash for v0.9.0

Ship **v0.8.0 now-ish** containing everything on `main`:

- **The substance (the planned theme):** your memory is now *durable* (backup/
  restore), *provable* (provenance, scope governance, conflict surfacing), and
  *conversational* (`om talk`). This is the story v0.8.0 was scoped to tell.
- **The preview (the excitement):** OM Mail ships clearly labeled
  **experimental** — agents with email addresses exchanging memory and
  negotiating recall. It earns a prominent "sneak peek" section, not the title.

Then reserve **v0.9.0 as the dedicated OM Mail release** once the live
AgentMail runbook has been validated end-to-end and Phase 1 ergonomics
(handshake tokens, scheduled sync, `om context` mail digest) land. Two
releases, two community moments: v0.8.0 says "om got serious about
trust and durability"; v0.9.0 says "agents can now mail each other memory" with
a demo that works in five minutes.

Why not hold mail out of v0.8.0: it is already merged, experimental-flagged,
default-off (no hooks, CLI-only), and 88-test covered — unshipping it buys
nothing. Why not call this v0.9.0 or lead with mail: the gate work deserves its
release identity, and mail's big moment is stronger after live multi-machine
validation than before it.

In order, after blockers clear and Bryan says go:

2. Write `docs/RELEASE-0.8.0.md` (skeleton in §6) and trim README's
   "Release State" section to the v0.8.0 story.
3. Bump `pyproject.toml` to `0.8.0`; sync the in-repo Homebrew formula

## 5. Announcement plan (the splash)

- **Positioning line:** "v0.8.0: your agent's memory is now durable, provable,
  and conversational — and (experimental) agents can email each other memory."
- **Assets:**
  - Release notes as the canonical artifact (GitHub Release + `docs/`).
  - A 60–90s terminal recording (asciinema) of the two-agent localdir demo
    from `docs/mail-memory.md`: send-note → accept → ask → answer. This is
    the shareable hook even though mail is the *preview*.
  - A short `om talk` clip — talking to your own memory is the feature
    casual users grasp instantly.
- **Channels:** GitHub Release + repo Discussions post (pin it), README badge
  bump, and Bryan's existing community/social channels. The Discussions post
  should explicitly invite mail-protocol feedback ("experimental — tell us
  where it breaks") to convert curiosity into design-partner conversations.
- **v0.9.0 teaser:** one sentence in the notes pointing at
  `plans/om-mail-next-steps.md` — handshake tokens, live listening, digests.
  Builds anticipation and frames the add-on boundary publicly stated there.
